2018 IN MUSIC

2018 saw new releases by Mitski, Pinegrove, Death Cab For Cutie, Julien Baker (with her new project, boygenius), and Taylor Swift - which may have something to do with the fact that all three cracked my personal top 10 artist list for 2018. In fact, looking at graph of my listening so far in 2018, you can see a spike in listening for Taylor Swift right in the middle of January 2018, when Reputation came out. But wait, that's not right. Reputation came out in November 2017. So what happened? I'm not exactly sure why I went on a TSwift kick (Look What You Made Me Do, Taylor) but that was apparently enough for me for the rest of the year. (In fact, you can see how much I listened to any particular artist by clicking on their name in the legend): I managed to see six out of my top ten bands live this year. I would mostly end up at the Sinclair, in Cambridge, MA: my most-listened to artist, Japanese Breakfast, was on June 1st, Pinegrove was on November 24th, and Tiny Moving Parts was on February 10th AND at the House of Blues in downtown Boston on November 25th. Mitski also performed at House of Blues earlier in the year, on October 20th. Boston Calling, May 23rd through 25th this year, featured both Julien Baker and The National.
